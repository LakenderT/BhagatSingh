Tribute to Bhagat Singh

BHAGHAT SINGH

Early Life and Education

Bhagat Singh was born on 28 September 1907 in Banga, Punjab, British India. He came from a family of revolutionaries and grew up in a politically charged environment. He was deeply influenced by the Jallianwala Bagh massacre of 1919 and the Non-Cooperation Movement launched by Mahatma Gandhi in 1920.

Bhagat Singh studied at the National College in Lahore, where he became involved in revolutionary politics. He joined the Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA) and became one of its leaders.

Revolutionary Activities

Bhagat Singh and his comrades in the HSRA believed in armed struggle against the British colonial government. They carried out several acts of sabotage and assassinations of British officials, including the killing of British police officer John Saunders in 1928.

In 1929, Bhagat Singh and two of his colleagues were arrested for the murder of Saunders. During their trial, they went on hunger strike to protest against the inhumane treatment of political prisoners. Despite widespread public support for their cause, they were sentenced to death and hanged on 23 March 1931.

Legacy

Bhagat Singh remains an icon of Indian nationalism and revolutionary socialism. He inspired generations of freedom fighters and socialists in India and around the world. His ideas and ideals continue to influence political and social movements to this day.

In his own words: "The sanctity of law can be maintained only so long as it is the expression of the will of the people."
